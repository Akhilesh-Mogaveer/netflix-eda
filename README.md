# Netflix Content Strategy Analysis 🎬

An end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of Netflix's content library (8,800+ titles),
uncovering patterns in content distribution, global expansion, audience targeting, and platform strategy.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- **Python** — Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn
- **Jupyter Notebook**
- **Dataset** — [Netflix Movies and TV Shows(Kaggle)]([https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shivamb/netflix-shows](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/rahulvyasm/netflix-movies-and-tv-shows))

---

## 🎯 Objective
To analyze Netflix's content library using data-driven techniques and extract actionable
business insights around content strategy, regional performance, audience targeting, and growth trends.

## 🔍 Key Findings

- **Movies dominate** at 69.6% of total content, but TV Shows are growing faster —
  signaling a shift toward engagement-driven strategy
- **USA contributes ~51%** of all content; India is 2nd at ~12%, reflecting heavy
  Bollywood investment
- **70%+ of content** is rated TV-MA or TV-14 — Netflix primarily targets mature audiences
- **67% of TV Shows** have only 1 season — suggesting limited series reliance or early cancellations
- **International Movies, Dramas, and Comedies** are the top 3 genres globally
- Content additions **peaked in 2019** (2,016 titles) and dropped in 2020, likely due to
  COVID-19 production shutdowns
